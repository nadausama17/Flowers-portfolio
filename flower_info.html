Hong Kong Orchid

It's the national flower of Hong Kong. It became a part of everything in this country. It's seen in the flag and coins. They are purple and red. Blooming season is from November to March.

Vanda ‘Miss Joaquim’

It's known as the Singapore Orchid or the Princess Aloha Orchid. It gets this name from the woman who found it. It was the national flower of Singapore in 1981. This flower gets 12 buds at a time, out of which mostly 4 blossom.

Cherry Blossom

It originated in the Himalayas, but now it's in many countries. Some types don't produce fruits. Sakura is a type of this flower and it's the national flower of Japan.

REFERENCE: Gardenerdy & Buzzle.com, < https://gardenerdy.com/list-of-asian-flowers-with-pictures/ >

Flame Lily

It's a climbing herb with glossy leaves. It grows from underground tuber which can survive during the dry season. The creeping stems die after fruiting.It grows in the north, east of South Africa,in Zimbabwe and east Africa. It's the national flower of Zimbabwe.

Leopard Orchid

It's an epiphytic orchid with roots on the branch of a tree. It's found in hot, dry river valleys of South Africa. Yellow flowers, spotted brown appear in August to September.

Wild Foxglove

It's a tall annual herb with erect stems. Flowers are pale pink or white. It occurs among rocks or along roadsides and in South Africa can be found in Mpumalanga and Limpopo provinces and in the Kruger National Park. Although beautiful, all parts of this plant are poisonous.

REFERENCE: Siyabona Africa, < http://www.krugerpark.co.za/Kruger_Park_Reference_Guide-travel/african-flowers-guide.html >

Sunflowers

They can be found in the south of France. They love warm and sunny weather. We can see rolling hillsides of sunflowers in late June in Dordogne region and from there to the Mediterranean coastline.

Edelweiss

Edelweiss grows at a high height among craggy rocks. We can see this flower in Switzerland and Austria. It's covered by dense hair which helps collect air bubbles and cause glowing sometimes in the sunshine. It's the symbol of Alps.

Lavender

Lavendar fields are synonymous with the South of France and Provence. The ideal time to explore it in June. The village of Sault is the capital of lavender.

REFERENCE: Culture trip, < https://theculturetrip.com/europe/france/articles/flowers-in-europe-the-best-blossoms-and-blooms-to-see-in-spring/ >

Desert Flame Chrysocephalum apiculatum

It brings a blaze of colour to the garden with golden yellow flowers and silvery grey foliage. It can grow 20–30cm tall. It prefers temperate climates and grows best in full sun or part shade. It grow in most soil types.

Pink rock lily Dendrobium

It produces flowers from pure white through to purple and pink. Dendrobiums like light but need shelter from midday sun. Tropical species need warmer conditions and more humidity. Dendrobiums will grow on rock faces or attached to tree branches.

Sturt's Desert Pea Swainsona formosa

It's the floral emblem for south Australia and famous for its eye-catching blood-red petals. It's difficult to be grown at home. It needs perfect drainage and full sun.

REFERENCE: Better Homes and Gardens, < https://www.bhg.com.au/5-climbing-plants-for-your-pergola >
